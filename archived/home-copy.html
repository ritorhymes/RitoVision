Despite her diverse talents as a model, musician, writer, and influencer, public perception often reduces her to a single facet of her identity. I saw and executed a vision for a clear and cohesive representation of Caroline's entire public image offering. This project serves as an example of how to create an immersive, engaging brand hub that brings everything together. JUMPTAG App

Knowledge Management System

Overview: The personalized Knowledge Management System (KMS) known as "EDUCATE" is a product-concept prototype that I envisioned to address common cognitive challenges in learning, retaining, and applying knowledge from various media sources. Inspired by Computer-Assisted Qualitative Data Analysis Software (CAQDAS) used in social sciences, the KMS aims to allows users to create a personalized repository of knowledge that can be cross-referenced and revisited, providing a deeper understanding of what they know and why they believe it. The project empowers users to manage their knowledge in a structured yet flexible way, facilitating critical thinking and reflection.

‍My Role: I initially conceived and developed the core concept for the Personalized KMS before bringing together a team of designers to further develop and refine the idea. As both the visionary and design lead, I guided the project from start to finish, identifying key pain points in traditional knowledge retention and proposing innovative solutions to extend users’ cognitive abilities. Collaborating closely with my team, we iterated through multiple design phases, ensuring that each step of the process aligned with the spirit and goals of the original concept, all while adhering to the technical requirements and constraints of the project.
"The medium is the message." -Marshall McLuhan'sUnderstanding Media: Extensions of Man

Inspiration & Theoretical RootsThe concept is inspired by the frustrations and inadequacies I have experienced during the research, study, and retention processes entailing the perpetual revisiting of content throughout my self-educating and academic endeavors.​The theoretical underpinnings are largely based on psychologist Lev Vygotsky's theory on sociocultural development whereby socially mediated processes are how people acquire their cultural values, beliefs, and problem-solving strategies through collaborative dialogues. The personalized media within this KMS (defined as media that have been annotated by the user) serve as socially mediating tools whereby the process of examining, reviewing, or editing is itself a social process impacting one's belief. Thus our notion of social processes may transcend the ordinary notion of a verbal conversation where instead dynamic media may sufficiently serve as a social catalyst medium with unique benefits and tradeoffs. Vygotsky's theory also entails that cultural symbols in various forms of consumable media are explicitly ingrained in our cognition such that the use of this kind of platform to integrate with daily thought can serve as a tool to extend cognition as naturally as the acquisition of meaningful words and sounds. In terms of the specific mechanisms applied in this prototype, our inspiration draws from Computer-Assisted Qualitative Data Analysis Software (CAQDAS) such as Atlas TI, MAXQDA, Transana. These are all software similar in function and often used by social scientists and researchers to compile data from qualitative media (books, videos, music, transcripts, articles, etc) to uncover insights and publish reports on findings. Our design is similar in spirit to some of the mechanics essential to virtually all CASQAD's, but utilized with a different intention in mind. Our design intends for the personalized content to be experientially consumed to serve as an auxiliary for learning such that it actually acts as a form of extended cognition. The key similarities are in the integration of various media sources into a single repository and, most crucially, the ability to annotate content which for our purpose enables us to personalize content towards an idiosyncratic knowledge base.
People have difficulty and experience confusion in reconciling separate media sources that use the same (or similar) key words with different definitions & contexts that are not explicitly clear enough of the time for the user to keep track of.​​The process of sorting through and reviewing material is cumbersome, especially when cross-referencing across disparate content.​​People don't have perfect recollection of the knowledge they acquired from sources and may either forget or distort certain facts or details.
I had been ideating this product concept for some time on my own; when our team was established I proposed the idea, introduced the problem pain-points, insights on potential solutions as well as shared the vision overall for what it could amount to in a full-fledged system at scale. I then coordinated with my fellow designers to ensure our designs and iterations lived up to the spirit of the idea with some essential technical requirements to adhere to but all within the scope of a short-term and resource-limited endeavor.

Call of Duty: Warzone

Quality Assurance Publicity Campaign

By leveraging my expertise in software usability analysis and my passion for artistic works like video games, I launched an independent public relations campaign to identify and highlight bugs (Quality Assurance) in the immensely popular online first-person shooter Call of Duty: Warzone. My content, which was picked up by various gaming news outlets, often led to these bugs being addressed and fixed by developers. This was at a time when the game studio managing the game had made severe cuts to its own internal QA team.
I uncovered a bug whereby certain parts of a popular map would result in player-controlled helicopters immediately self-destructing when making contact with the ground. I created a music video montage effectively capturing demonstrations of this bug in action and detailed pretty much every spot on the map where this took place. The content was picked up by gaming news outlets such as Dexerto, Charlie Intel, GameRant and the bug was swiftly fixed thereafter.
